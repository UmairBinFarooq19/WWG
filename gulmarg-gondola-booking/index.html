Essential Info

Gulmarg Gondola — Everything You Need to Know

🕐 Timing & Availability

  • Operating hours: 10:00 AM – 4:00 PM (last ride)
  • Closed on Tuesdays for maintenance
  • Phase 1 operates year-round (weather permitting)
  • Phase 2 closed during heavy snowfall (Jan–Feb)
  • Book at least 2 days in advance in peak season

👥 Who Can Ride?

  • Minimum age: 5 years
  • Children under 12: concessional rates available
  • Senior citizens (60+): discounted tickets
  • No weight restriction
  • Pregnant women advised to skip Phase 2

🎿 Peak Season Tips

  • December–March: book 3–5 days in advance
  • Weekends sell out fastest — book by Thursday
  • Early morning slots (10–11 AM) have clearest views
  • Carry warm layers — Apharwat is -20°C in winter
  • Altitude sickness possible at Phase 2 (3,979m)

📍 Getting to the Gondola

  • Gondola station is in Gulmarg village centre
  • From Srinagar: 56km via Tangmarg (~2 hrs)
  • Parking available at Gulmarg market
  • WildWays provides Srinagar–Gulmarg taxi
  • No public bus to Gulmarg — private cab essential
Common Questions

Gulmarg Gondola FAQs

Gulmarg Gondola Phase 1 ticket costs ₹900/person (Gulmarg to Kongdoori, 3,050m). Phase 2 costs ₹1,050/person (Kongdoori to Apharwat Peak, 3,979m). Both phases combined: ₹1,850/person. Children under 12 and senior citizens get concessional rates. Prices are set by the J&K Cable Car Corporation and WildWays Global does not charge any markup.

Gulmarg Gondola tickets can be booked online through the official SKIMS/J&K Cable Car Corporation website, or through authorised agents like WildWays Global. Online booking is recommended in peak seasons (Dec–Jan and May–Jun) to avoid sold-out situations. WildWays Global handles online booking on your behalf as part of all Gulmarg tour packages.

If visiting for the first time, we recommend doing both phases. Phase 1 offers beautiful village and meadow views and access to ski slopes, while Phase 2 takes you to Apharwat Peak (3,979m) with views of Nanga Parbat and Himalayan glaciers. However, if you have altitude sensitivity, Phase 1 alone is also a fantastic experience. Phase 2 is NOT recommended for people with heart conditions or severe altitude sickness.

Yes! Phase 1 of the Gulmarg Gondola operates year-round (weather permitting). Phase 2 also operates in summer (April–November) with clear Himalayan views. In summer, the gondola takes you over lush green meadows instead of snow. Operations pause occasionally due to high winds or technical maintenance. Always check current status before your visit — WildWays Global provides real-time updates to all booked guests.

Phase 1 (Gulmarg to Kongdoori) takes approximately 6 minutes. Phase 2 (Kongdoori to Apharwat Peak) takes approximately 8 minutes. Plan to spend 2–4 hours total at the gondola, including boarding time, photo stops, and time at the summit. In peak season, factor in queuing time of 30–60 minutes unless you have advance booking.
